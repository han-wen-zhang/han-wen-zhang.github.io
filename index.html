Hanwen Zhang


I am a Gibbs Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at Yale University. My research focuses on fast and accurate algorithms for computational tasks in physics by applying techniques from modern applied mathematics. These techniques resolve around numerical methods for integral equations and analysis-based fast algorithms.

Previouly, I completed my PhD in Applied Physics at Yale and my advisor was Owen Miller. I received a BS in Physics and a BE in Material Science both with highest distinction from the National University of Singapore.
